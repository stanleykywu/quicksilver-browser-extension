# Quicksilver
A fast Rust + WASM based browser extension that can quickly identify AI-generated music by directly processing music audio from any online streaming source (YouTube, Spotify, Apple Music, ..., etc.). In other words, if your browser can play it, Quicksilver can directly classify the audio without downloading it. If you are looking for development setup, please look [here](#setup)

## What is Quicksilver?

AI-generated music has grown incredibly quickly since 2024. Since then, our research has found that up to half of new songs uploaded to platforms like Spotify are now AI. Much of these AI songs are low quality, low effort AI music, with many even posing as legitimate, human musicians. In response, AI music detection has been proposed as a way to label and inform listeners on what songs are AI or not. However, as of this writing, the only place where AI songs are labeled this way is on the Deezer platform. While we believe this is a step in the right direction for AI transparency, we believe users should have access to the necessary tools to easily determine whether any given music is AI-generated or not, and not just that uploaded to certain platforms.

In an effort to address this, we have designed and implemented Quicksilver, a fast and low latency browser extension, which when given permission by the user, can record any audio from the browser (currently requiring 30 seconds), and quickly identify if it contains AI-generated music artifacts. Our detector is a lightweight (only 37KB!) machine learning model based on the open-source detector that [Deezer’s research team released](https://github.com/deezer/ismir25-ai-music-detector), which is the best performing model we tested in our latest research paper. In our testing, we found that our model is 99.8% accurate on our test set of both Suno and Udio generated songs, with a false positive rate of 0.1%.

### Limitations
1. Our model depends heavily on the AI songs it is trained on. Version 1.0 is only trained to detect AI songs generated by Udio v1.5 and Suno v5. Songs generated by (potentially) newer versions of Suno/Udio may not be classified as AI. We will attempt to update our model if and when new versions are released.
2. Our model is not trained to detect open source models like DiffRhythm or ACE-Step. Based on our research paper, it does not seem like many people are using these models to generate and publish music (yet).
3. We do not claim our model is robust to audio augmentations like pitch shift, reverb, … etc. We are actively working to improve the robustness of our model, but we note here that motivated adversaries can intentionally edit their AI songs to avoid detection.
 
### What makes Quicksilver different from online detectors like SubmitHub, Hive, …, etc.?
The goal of Quicksilver is to be fast, and require as little effort as possible to use. As such, instead of requiring users to first download music files, then upload to an online service, our detector directly listen to music that is streamed online in your browser tab, and classifies it. Additionally, since the model is lightweight, it runs directly on your computer without needing the audio file to be sent anywhere. Finally, we also open sourced our code on GitHub, and are always open to improvements, suggestions, and comments.

### Does Quicksilver work on audio deepfakes?
Our model is trained to detect Suno and Udio generated music, which represents a bulk of AI songs on streaming platforms. If the deepfake was created using one of those tools, our model should be able to detect it. However, our model is not trained to detect pure speech generated by AI, since those use different models other than Suno/Udio.
